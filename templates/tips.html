Self-Care Troubleshooting

Follow these best practices to improve the speed, security, and performance of your SmartApp Wi-Fi network.

Best Practices

Do these to maximize your fiber experience

  • Place your Wi-Fi router in a central, open location for optimal coverage.
  • Keep your router elevated (on a table or shelf), never on the floor.
  • Use a strong, unpredictable password (WPA3 or WPA2) to secure your network.
  • Restart your router once a week to clear cache and improve performance.
  • Regularly update your router's firmware for the latest security patches.
  • Use the 5GHz band for devices close to the router to achieve maximum speed.

Things to Avoid

Don't do these to prevent connection drops

  • Don't place the router near thick concrete walls, fish tanks, or large metal appliances.
  • Don't share your primary Wi-Fi password; set up a 'Guest Network' for visitors instead.
  • Don't enclose the router inside closed TV cabinets or drawers (it causes overheating).
  • Don't keep the router near microwaves or cordless phones to prevent signal interference.
  • Don't use outdated or cheap secondary routers, as they will bottleneck your fiber speed.
